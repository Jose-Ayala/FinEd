Personal Finance Basics

Basic Concepts & Key Terms

In this lesson, you will understand and apply basic personal finance key terms such as income, expenses, budget, savings, account, debt, investing, credit score, and interest. Let's begin.

What is Personal Finance?

All financial decisions and activities of an individual or household. In other words, planning how to spend your own money is personal finance.

Income

Income is the money you earn from different sources like allowances, part-time jobs, or gifts. Itâ€™s important because it helps you pay for things you need now and save for your future.

Income = The money you earn.

Expenses

The money you spend on things you need, like food, housing, and transportation, as well as things you choose to spend on, like entertainment and hobbies.

Expenses = The money you spend.

Budget

A plan that helps you track your income and expenses, so you can manage your money better and achieve your goals.

Budget = Your plan for your money.

Savings Account

A bank account where you can deposit money and earn interest, usually used to save for future needs or emergencies.

Savings Account = Your adult piggy bank.

Debt

Money borrowed from lenders that you must pay back, usually with interest. Common types of debt include student loans, credit card balances, and mortgages.

Debt = The money you borrow.
