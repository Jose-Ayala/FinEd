What is a Bank?

A bank is a place where people keep their money safe.

Think of it like a big, secure piggy bank. Instead of keeping your money at home, you can put it in a bank. Banks help you save money, spend it easily with things like debit cards, and even borrow money if you need to buy something big.

Deposit Accounts

A deposit account is a type of bank account where you can put your money to keep it safe and earn interest. There are different kinds of deposit accounts, like checking accounts, savings accounts, and CDs (Certificates of Deposit). The main idea is that you deposit (put) your money into the account, and the bank keeps it safe for you. Depending on the type of account, you can use the money for everyday spending, save it for the future, or earn higher interest by leaving it in the account for a set period.

Checking Accounts

A checking account is like your everyday money account. It's where you keep the money you need for daily expenses. You can use it to pay for things with a debit card, write checks, or withdraw cash from an ATM. It's super convenient because you can access your money anytime you need it.

Savings Accounts

A savings account is for money you want to save up for the future. It's like a safe place to stash your cash where it can grow over time. You earn a little bit of extra money called interest just for keeping your money there. It's great for saving up for big things like a new phone, college, or a car.

Certificates of Deposit

A Certificate of Deposit (CD) account is a special type of savings account. You agree to leave your money in the bank for a set period of time, like 6 months or a year, and the bank gives you a higher interest rate. It's a good way to earn more money on your savings, but you can't touch the money until the time is up without paying a penalty.

Retirement Accounts

A retirement account is a special type of account where you save money for the future — when you're older and ready to stop working. These accounts help you put money aside now, so you'll have it later when you need it.

Traditional IRA

You put money into this account and get a tax break now, meaning you don’t pay taxes on the money you contribute until you take it out later when you retire. This is a good option if you want to save more money up front.

Roth IRA

With a Roth IRA, you put money in after you’ve already paid taxes on it. The good part? When you take the money out in the future, you don’t have to pay any taxes on it. This is great if you think your taxes will be higher when you’re older.

401(k)

A 401(k) is like a retirement account that your employer offers. You can have part of your paycheck automatically sent into this account, and sometimes your employer will even add money to it for you. Just like an IRA, you’re saving money now for when you retire.
